The Chaurasan Sidhi Temple

The Chaurasan Sidhi Temple is a historical and religious site dedicated to Lord Shiva within the Rohtasgarh Fort. Its name, 'Chaurasan Sidhi,' literally means '84 Steps,' referring to the unique staircase carved out of stone that leads visitors to the shrine. This pathway highlights the temple's antiquity and unique structural feature. The remaining stairs and temple ruins clearly demonstrate the fort's ancient heritage and its peaceful, spiritual significance.

The Indrapuri Dam

The Indrapuri Barrage, located on the Son River in the Rohtas district, is a lifeline for agriculture in South Bihar. Commissioned in 1968, this barrage is approximately 1,407 meters long, making it one of the world's longest barrages. Its primary function is to supply water for irrigation across several districts like Rohtas and Bhojpur through the historic Son Canal System, which has turned the region into a major rice producer. Besides its functional importance, the barrage is also a scenic spot, drawing tourists with its picturesque views of the wide Son River, especially during sunset.

Manjhar Kund

The Manjhar Kund is a captivating cascade waterfall and a well-known natural tourist spot situated on the Kaimur Hills near Sasaram in the Rohtas district. Fed by the Kav River, the waterfall's beauty is at its peak during the monsoon season. Beyond its stunning natural scenery, the site holds significant religious importance; a large traditional fair is held here on the first Sunday after Raksha Bandhan, and it was historically a sacred stopping point for the Sikh community. The cool, mineral-rich water of the cascade makes it a perfect destination for picnics and nature enthusiasts looking for an adventurous escape.

Rohtasgarh Fort

The Rohtasgarh Fort is a massive and historic hill fort situated on the Kaimur Hills in the Rohtas district. This fort was once a vital military center for rulers like Sher Shah Suri and Raja Man Singh. Its vast perimeter, grand gateways like the Hathiya Pol, and palace ruins showcase its glorious architecture. Separately, Manjhar Kund is a picturesque natural waterfall located near Sasaram, famous for its beauty and cool, mineral-rich water. The natural scenery here is most spectacular during the monsoon, and along with the nearby Dhuan Kund, it attracts tourists and picnic enthusiasts. The history of Rohtasgarh and the natural beauty of Manjhar Kund collectively define the unique heritage of Rohtas.

Karwandiya: The KGF of Bihar

The Karwandiya Mines are a large stone quarrying area located near Sasaram in the Rohtas district of Bihar. It is unofficially nicknamed "The KGF of Bihar." This name comes from the area's dramatic and cinematic appearance. Extensive mining operations have left behind deep, towering rocky trenches and steep cliffs. Rainwater has collected in these deep pits, creating large, turquoise-colored quarry lakes. This rugged landscape strongly resembles the fictional mining site shown in the famous movie K.G.F. Today, the site has become a unique and popular spot for photography and travel vlogging.

Tutla Bhawani (Temple)

Tutla Bhawani is a famous religious and scenic tourist destination located near Tilouthu in the Rohtas district of Bihar. This site is renowned for both an ancient temple of Maa Tutla Bhawani and a magnificent natural waterfall. The waterfall cascades from a height of about 180 to 200 meters, making the location very picturesque. The temple is nestled within a lush green valley among the Kaimur Hills. The beauty of the waterfall is at its peak during the monsoon season, when the water flow is strongest. A suspension bridge has also been built for better access to the temple, making it an excellent spot for eco-tourism.

Maa Tarachandi Dham(Temple)

Tara Chandi Dham is an ancient temple located in Sasaram, Rohtas district, Bihar. It is considered one of the 51 Shakti Peethas in India. According to legends, the right eye of Goddess Sati fell at this sacred spot. The temple is built inside a natural cave in the Kaimur Hills. The idol of Goddess Tarachandi is uniquely installed in the form of a girl child. A huge crowd of devotees visits the shrine during Navratri. This site is famous for both its religious importance and natural beauty.

The Tomb of Sher Shah Suri

The Tomb of Sher Shah Suri is an extraordinary historical monument located in Sasaram, Bihar. It is the final resting place of Sher Shah Suri, the founder of the Sur Empire, constructed between 1540 and 1545 AD. The mausoleum is a superb example of Indo-Islamic architecture, designed by the architect Mir Muhammad Aliwal Khan. Its most unique feature is its location right in the center of a vast, artificial lake covering about 22 acres. This grand, three-storied octagonal structure is built from red sandstone and stands approximately 122 feet tall. Due to its majestic scale and water-based setting, it is often referred to as the "Second Taj Mahal of India."
